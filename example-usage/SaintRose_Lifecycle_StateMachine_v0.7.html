Participant lifecycle state machine

v0.7

Sample document used: 2019 Plan Document.pdf. This artifact is the lifecycle / entitlement state machine for the Saint Rose plan backbone. It is designed to answer: what state is the person in, what event occurred, and what promise becomes available next?

Read left to right. The most important structural split is: participation → vesting / retirement eligibility → commencement → death / suspension / survivor consequences.

Interpretive notes

What this artifact makes explicit
1. Participation creates the active state.
2. Excluded-class changes create the inactive state.
3. Termination before three years leads to no benefit.
4. Termination after three years creates the vested deferred branch.
5. Early retirement = age 55 plus 10 Years of Service.
6. Normal retirement = age 65; deferred retirement is later commencement after that date.
7. Disability retirement is its own branch.
8. Death logic splits before-commencement and after-commencement.
9. Reemployment after commencement triggers benefit suspension logic.

State meanings

Eligible Employee
Employee who satisfies plan-eligibility conditions but has not yet been shown here as an Active Participant.
Active Participant
Eligible Employee participating in the Plan. Source: §1.26(a), §2.1.
Inactive Participant
Former Active Participant who is no longer active because of status/class change while still employed. Source: §1.26(b), §2.2.
Vested Participant / (deferred / not fully distributed)
Current or former Eligible Employee, not yet retired, with vested Accrued Benefit not fully distributed. Source: §1.26(d), §4.4, §5.3.
Early Retirement / Eligible
Participant at age 55 with 10 Years of Service. Source: §1.32(c), §4.3, §5.1.
Normal / Deferred / Retirement Eligible
Participant at age 65 or later deferral. Source: §1.32(a),(b),(d), §4.1, §4.2, §5.1.
Disability Retirement / Eligible
Active or Inactive Participant with Disability and 10 Years of Service. Source: §1.32(d), §4.3, §5.2.
Retired Participant / / In Pay
Former Eligible Employee whose employment terminated and who is eligible for or receiving retirement benefit. Source: §1.26(c), §5.1, §8.3.
Benefit Suspended on / Reemployment
Participant reemployed after benefit commencement; monthly retirement benefit may be suspended. Source: §5.5.
Beneficiary Before / Commencement
Pre-commencement survivor stream for spouse / beneficiary when death occurs before retirement income begins. Source: §7.1, §7.3.
Beneficiary After / Commencement
Post-commencement survivor or residual-payment stream. Source: §7.2.
No Plan Benefit
Absorbing no-benefit outcome where plan promises nothing under the specified trigger. Source: §5.3(a), opening sentence of §7.1.
